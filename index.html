Yang Xin

Greetings! My name is Yang Xin (辛杨).

I am a Ph.D. student working on computer vision, face recognition, EEG-based visual decoding, and neural representation learning. My research focuses on learning robust and discriminative visual representations from both image data and brain signals.

In computer vision, I have worked on robust face recognition under noisy and weakly-cleaned large-scale datasets, as well as structured identity representation learning for challenging scenarios such as pose variation, occlusion, illumination changes, and similar-identity recognition.

In brain-inspired visual perception, I study whether human face identity perception can be decoded from non-invasive EEG signals. My recent work includes face-evoked EEG dataset construction, high-density EEG signal processing, identity-level neural decoding, and topology-spectral deep learning models for EEG visual representation learning.

Overall, my research aims to bridge visual perception, face identity representation, and neural decoding, with the goal of understanding and modeling how identity information is represented in both artificial vision systems and human brain signals.
