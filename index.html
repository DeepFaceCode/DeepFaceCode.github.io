Yang Xin

Greetings! My name is Yang Xin (辛杨).

I am currently a Ph.D. student in Artificial Intelligence. My research interests include EEG-based Visual Decoding, Brain-Computer Interface, Deep Learning, Face Recognition, and Neural Representation Learning.

I am a Ph.D. student working on brain-inspired visual perception, EEG neural decoding, and deep learning. My research focuses on understanding whether and how human visual perception, especially face identity perception, can be decoded from non-invasive EEG signals.

My recent work explores face-evoked EEG representation learning, identity-level neural decoding, high-density EEG dataset construction, and topology-spectral modeling for robust EEG visual decoding. I also have research experience in robust face recognition, structured identity representation learning.
